1 Define the System

Master the foundation of systems thinking by learning to establish clear boundaries and identify the essential elements of any system.

üéØ

Why System Definition Matters

Poor system definition is the #1 reason why solutions fail. Without clear boundaries, you'll:

‚ùå Solve the wrong problem
‚ùå Miss critical stakeholders
‚ùå Create solutions that don't stick
‚úÖ Good system definition ensures you're working on the right problem with the right scope.

Core Concepts to Master

üî≤ Boundaries

What's inside vs outside your system. This determines your scope of influence and responsibility.

üéØ Purpose

Why the system exists. Every system serves a function - sometimes multiple conflicting ones.

üëÅÔ∏è Perspective

Different stakeholders see different systems. A hospital is a business to investors, healing to patients.

6-Step System Definition Framework

1
Start with the Problem

What specific issue are you trying to solve? Be concrete.

2
Identify the Purpose

What function does this system serve? What's its reason for existing?

3
Map Key Players

Who are the main actors? Include people, organizations, and roles.

4
Set Boundaries

What's in vs out? Use your sphere of influence as a guide.

5
Consider Perspectives

How do different stakeholders view this system differently?

6
Test & Refine

Does this definition help you solve the original problem?

3 Identify Feedback Loops

Discover how actions create reactions that influence the original action, creating cycles of reinforcement or balance.

üîÑ

Why Feedback Loops Are The Secret to Systems Behavior

Feedback loops create the dynamic behavior patterns that make systems come alive. Understanding them reveals:

üéØ Why problems keep recurring despite "solutions"
üéØ How small actions can create exponential change
üéØ Where systems naturally resist or amplify change
‚ö†Ô∏è Without understanding loops: You're fighting symptoms while the pattern continues.

2 Fundamental Loop Types That Drive Everything

üöÄ Reinforcing Loops (R)

Growth engines or death spirals
Whatever is happening accelerates. Success breeds success, problems compound.

Behavior: Exponential growth or decline
Virtuous: Exercise ‚Üí Energy ‚Üí More Exercise
Vicious: Stress ‚Üí Poor Decisions ‚Üí More Stress
‚öñÔ∏è Balancing Loops (B)

Goal-seeking stabilizers
System seeks equilibrium, counteracts deviations from target state.

Behavior: Oscillation toward target
Helpful: Thermostat maintaining temperature
Limiting: Organizational resistance to change

Advanced Loop Dynamics

‚è±Ô∏è Delays

Time gaps between cause and effect create instability and overshooting.

Example: Hiring lag ‚Üí Overwork ‚Üí Burnout ‚Üí More hiring needed
üëë Loop Dominance

Multiple loops compete. The strongest loop determines system behavior.

Example: Growth ambition vs. resource limits
üîÑ Loop Shifts

Systems switch between different dominant loops over time.

Example: Startup: Growth loops ‚Üí Maturity: Efficiency loops

5-Step Framework for Loop Detection

Use this systematic approach to uncover hidden feedback loops in any situation:

1
Start with a Behavior Pattern

What recurring pattern puzzles you? Growth, decline, oscillation, or stagnation?

2
Trace the Causal Chain

Follow cause and effect: A influences B, which influences C, which influences...

3
Find Where It Circles Back

Does the chain eventually influence the original element? That's your loop!

4
Identify Loop Type & Polarity

Reinforcing (amplifies) or Balancing (seeks goal)? Positive or negative effects?

5
Look for Delays & Dominance

What delays hide the connection? Which loop dominates when multiple loops compete?

‚ö†Ô∏è Symptom Looping

Problem: Confusing symptoms with actual feedback loops

4 Understand Stocks, Flows & Delays

Recognize what accumulates (stocks), rates of change (flows), and time delays in the system.

Key Concepts:

  • Stocks: What accumulates (water in bathtub, knowledge, money)
  • Flows: Rates of change (inflow/outflow, learning rate)
  • Delays: Time between cause and effect

Examples:

üß†
Knowledge System: Knowledge (stock) + Learning rate (flow) + Application delay
üí∞
Financial System: Bank balance (stock) + Income/expenses (flows)
‚è±Ô∏è
Delays: Training today ‚Üí Performance improvement in 3 months

6 Find Leverage Points

Identify where small shifts in one thing can produce big changes in everything - the key to effective systems intervention.

Leverage Hierarchy (Increasing Power):

  • Parameters & Flows: Numbers, budgets, subsidies
  • Rules & Information: Policies, feedback loops, transparency
  • Power & Goals: Who decides, what we optimize for
  • Paradigms: Mindsets, worldviews, beliefs
